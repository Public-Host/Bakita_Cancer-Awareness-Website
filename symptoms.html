Signs and Symptoms of Cancer

General Symptoms of Cancer

  • Unexplained Weight Loss: Losing more than 10% of body weight without trying, often seen in advanced stages of cancer.
  • Fatigue: Persistent fatigue that does not improve with rest.
  • Pain: Persistent or unexplained pain, especially in areas where a tumor is pressing on nerves or organs.
  • Fever: A persistent fever, often with no other explanation, especially in advanced cancer.
  • Skin Changes: Changes like jaundice (yellowing), darkening, or the development of new moles or changes in existing ones.

Localized Symptoms

  • Breast Changes: Lumps, changes in size or shape of the breast, or unusual pain/discharge from the nipple.
  • Bowel or Bladder Changes: Blood in stools, difficulty urinating, or changes in bowel habits.
  • Cough or Difficulty Breathing: Persistent cough, shortness of breath, or coughing up blood.
  • Swollen Lymph Nodes: Lymph nodes that feel swollen or tender, especially in the neck, armpit, or groin.
  • Difficulty Swallowing: Pain while swallowing, or a feeling of food getting stuck in the throat.
  • Mouth or Throat Changes: Persistent sore throat, mouth ulcers, or pain that doesn't heal.

Symptoms Related to Blood and Immune System

  • Unexplained Bleeding: Bleeding gums, bruising, or prolonged bleeding could be a sign of cancer.
  • Anemia: Fatigue, weakness, and dizziness due to low red blood cell count.
  • Infections: Recurring or persistent infections that are difficult to treat.

Other Symptoms Specific to Cancer Types

  • Jaundice: Yellowing of the skin and eyes, common with liver or pancreatic cancer.
  • Abdominal Swelling or Pain: This may indicate ovarian, liver, or pancreatic cancer.
  • Hoarseness or Changes in Voice: Can be a sign of throat or laryngeal cancer.

7 Warning Signs of Cancer (American Cancer Society)

  • Change in Bowel or Bladder Habits
  • A Sore That Does Not Heal
  • Unusual Bleeding or Discharge
  • Thickening or Lump in the Breast or Elsewhere
  • Indigestion or Difficulty Swallowing
  • Obvious Change in a Wart or Mole
  • Persistent Cough or Hoarseness

When to Seek Medical Attention

If any of the above symptoms persist or worsen, or if you have concerns about cancer, it is important to consult a healthcare provider for further evaluation. Early detection can lead to better outcomes and treatment options.
